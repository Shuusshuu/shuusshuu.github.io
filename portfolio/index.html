Life Insurance Industry - Customer Business Transformation Report in the Digital Age

PricewaterhouseCoopers Mainland China and Hong Kong

During the “14th Five-Year Plan” period, China has entered a new stage of development, which is characterised by networking, informatisation, digitisation and intelligence. Under the guidance of the “Digital China” development strategy, various industries are gradually advancing digital transformation, and the era of “digital finance” has begun.

Against this background, China’s life insurance industry is undergoing extensive and profound changes. This change stems not only from the objective requirements of the industry entering a new stage and requiring deeper development, but also from the rapid changes in the external ecological environment and the guidance and drive of national policies. Digital transformation has become the core force driving transformational change.

Mapping out virus and natural hazards footprint via NO2 tracing using remote sensing data

National Computational Infrastructure Australia

Nitrogen oxide (NO2) is a precursor chemical that contributes to the formation of ozone (O3) and fine particulate matter (PM2.5), which are the indicators of air pollution. Satellite imageries extracted from the Sentinel-5 Precursor (Sentinal-5P) provide data on the NO2 concentration in the atmosphere. This data source gives a direct measurement of the NO2 footprint globally. The global pandemic impacts people’s lifestyles to a large extent, including their commutation, business travel and tourism. The change in traffic and travel frequency further impacts the air pollution status, which can be quantified by mapping NO2 in a time series format. Hence it creates a linkage between human activities and their environmental impacts. In addition, natural hazards such as bushfire affect human’s life and emit a large amount of green gas during burning. We aim to quantify the linkage between the environmental impact and those events in both time and space perspectives using satellite imagery products. The linkage demonstrated in various locations reflects the local social and economic activities through the quantified environmental impact data analysis approach. In particular, we use Sentinal-5P satellite images to monitor air quality before and after COVID to see the air quality variation. We apply the same approach to evaluate the amount of NO2 emission during the bushfire season in Australia. This is a direct measurement of the social and environmental impact using the Earth observation data. The data is downloaded from the NASA Earthdata web portal. A number of satellite image processing tools and libraries are used for data analysis.
